Why it matters

A pass/fail score tells you something broke. Veralith tells you what broke, where, and exactly how to fix it — for every trace your RAG serves.

The loop

From a silent failure to a merged fix.

Four moves. You only ever touch the first one and review the last.

01 · Instrument

One line

Drop veralith.log(...) — or the @trace decorator, or the LangChain adapter for zero code changes.

02 · Diagnose

Decompose & judge

Server-side, Veralith splits query and answer into atoms, runs three LLM-judge metrics, and classifies the trace.

03 · See it

A typed diagnosis

Traces stream into the dashboard — health, latency, knowledge gaps, and the 2×3 failure-cell breakdown.

04 · Heal it

Your agent's PR

Recurring failures cluster into a heal card; your coding agent claims it over MCP and opens a pull request.

Six failure modes, not one flag

Different bugs need different fixes.

Cross-tab Completeness against Faithfulness and every trace lands in a named cell — so you read the fix, not a lookup chart.

Sufficiency — was retrieval good enough?
Faithfulness — is each claim grounded?
Completeness — did it answer all of it?
✅
complete_grounded
Answers everything, every claim supported. The healthy trace.
⚠︎
complete_ungrounded
Full answer, but invented a claim. Classic hallucination.
incomplete_grounded
Grounded, but skipped part of the query. Retrieval gap.
⚠︎
incomplete_ungrounded
Missed part and invented a claim. Worst case.
extra_grounded
Over-answered with grounded but unrequested content.
extra_ungrounded
Padded with unsupported extra content. Scope creep.

The part that isn't generic

Fixes written inside your own repo.
